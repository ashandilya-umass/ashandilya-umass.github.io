1. Hydroxyl (-OH): This group makes molecules like alcohols and sugars soluble in water and helps them react to form new compounds. 2. Carbonyl (C=O): Found in molecules like aldehydes and ketones, this group makes them useful for building larger molecules in living organisms. 3. Carboxyl (-COOH): This group makes molecules like vinegar sour and helps them react with other substances to form new compounds. 4. Amino (-NH2): Found in molecules like amines, this group can act like a magnet for protons, making molecules more reactive. 5. Phosphate (-PO4H2): This group is like a battery in cells, storing and transferring energy for various processes. 6. Sulfhydryl (-SH): This group helps hold proteins together and can be involved in chemical reactions inside cells. 7. Methyl (-CH3): This group is like a tag that can change the properties of a molecule, affecting how it behaves in the body. 8. Ethers (-O-): Ethers are like a pair of arms that can link two parts of a molecule together, making them useful in making plastics and medicines. 9. Halogen (-X): This group can act like a handle, making it easier to break a molecule apart or attach something new. 10. Nitro (-NO2): Found in explosives and medicines, this group can make molecules more reactive and sometimes explosive. 11. Amide (-CONH2): This group is found in proteins and helps them fold into their correct shape, allowing them to perform their functions in the body.
